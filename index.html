01 — What is Theta Healing

A gateway to the subconscious mind

ThetaHealing® is a meditative technique and spiritual philosophy founded in 1995. It combines science and spirituality to identify and change subconscious beliefs that may be holding you back — emotionally, physically, and spiritually. Sessions take place in the theta brainwave state (4–8 Hz), the same state reached in deep meditation and during the threshold of sleep.

01

The Theta state

Theta brainwaves (4–8 Hz) are associated with deep relaxation, heightened creativity, and access to the subconscious — where deeply held beliefs live.

02

Belief work

Limiting beliefs — often formed in childhood or past experiences — are identified, explored, and replaced with empowering ones through a gentle co-creative process.

03

Holistic healing

Theta Healing addresses the whole person: emotional blocks, physical symptoms, relationship patterns, abundance, and spiritual connection — all are interrelated.

03 — Who is it for

Is this for you?

Theta Healing is for anyone open to exploring the deeper layers of themselves. There are no prerequisites — only a willingness to look within.

About Theta Healing

ThetaHealing® is a meditative technique developed by Vianna Stibal in 1995. It works by guiding both the practitioner and client into the theta brainwave state (4–8 Hz) — the same state experienced in deep meditation and just before sleep.

In this state, the subconscious mind becomes accessible. Anjali will guide you to identify beliefs that may be limiting your life — whether in relationships, health, abundance, or self-worth — and facilitate a shift using focused intention and connection to a higher energy.

Think of it as a compassionate conversation with the deeper layers of yourself — with Anjali as your guide.

The theta brainwave state itself is well-documented in neuroscience research. The specific claims of ThetaHealing® as a healing modality are not yet the subject of large-scale clinical trials, as with many complementary practices.

Theta Healing is considered a complementary and holistic practice — it works alongside conventional medicine and therapy, not as a replacement. Many clients report meaningful shifts in how they feel and think after sessions, though individual results vary.
